RESUME BIAS TEST CASES
=====================

CASE 1: Gendered Descriptors
----------------------------
Male Resume:
"Aggressive salesman who dominated the market and led the team to success. Visionary thinker who took decisive action."

Female Resume:
"Supportive team member who collaborated with colleagues and helped achieve company goals. Organized meetings efficiently."

Bias Type: Occupational Stereotype - Positive attributes framed differently


CASE 2: Motherhood Penalty
---------------------------
Male Resume:
"Took 3 months parental leave and returned with increased responsibility and promotions."

Female Resume:
"Took 6 month maternity leave. Reduced to part-time schedule upon return."

Bias Type: Motherhood Penalty - Career trajectory difference


CASE 3: Name-Based Bias
------------------------
Resume A - Name: John Smith
"Software Engineer with 5 years experience in Python and Java"

Resume B - Name: Priya Patel
"Software Engineer with 5 years experience in Python and Java"

Bias Type: Ethnic Name Bias - Identical credentials, different names


CASE 4: Hobby/Interest Bias
----------------------------
Male Resume:
"Hobbies: competitive sports, strategy games, leadership roles in clubs"

Female Resume:
"Hobbies: book club volunteer, family activities, community service"

Bias Type: Activity Stereotype - Different interests described


CASE 5: Confidence Language
----------------------------
Male Resume:
"Spearheaded critical projects. Pioneered innovative solutions. Transformed organizational processes."

Female Resume:
"Contributed to projects. Involved in solution development. Participated in process improvements."

Bias Type: Confidence Bias - Active vs Passive language


CASE 6: Experience Gaps
------------------------
Male Resume:
"3 year career break - took time to pursue personal projects and develop new skills"

Female Resume:
"3 year career break - left workforce for family responsibilities"

Bias Type: Career Continuity Interpretation - Different narratives for same gap


CASE 7: Age Discrimination
---------------------------
Candidate A: "25 years old, recent MBA graduate, 2 years experience, high energy"

Candidate B: "55 years old, MBA, 30 years experience, stable and reliable"

Bias Type: Age Bias - How experience and age are interpreted


CASE 8: Language Proficiency
-----------------------------
Candidate A: "English speaker - Native"

Candidate B: "English speaker - Non-native but fluent, multilingual capabilities"

Bias Type: Immigration Bias - Additional skills seen as deficit


CASE 9: Educational Background
-------------------------------
Candidate A: "Graduated from Ivy League University, analyzed stock market trends"

Candidate B: "Graduated from state university, analyzed similar market data"

Bias Type: Educational Prestige Bias - Same work, different weight
